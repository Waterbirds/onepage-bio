Internship Project

Castlepoint's Business Problem

For Castlepoint to apply the correct regulations to document, Castlepoint must understand the content of the documents. This is complicated by the size of the datasets maintained by Castlepoint clients which is often in the Terabyte to Petabyte range, as well as the many different combinations of topics stored within the datasets. Extensive compute resources and time is required to understand the data at these scales.


Internship Goal

The goal of the internship project was to research and produce reports and implementations of various Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Machine Learning (ML) models. Providing Castlepoint with the necessary information to make an informed decision, when choosing which NLP & ML methods to implement into their SaaS records management product. Helping an organisation apply the correct rules, regulations, and appropriate lifecycle controls to their record databases.



Key Phrase Extraction

  • Researched a multitude of natural language processing and key phrase extraction models. Selecting the best models based on speed, resource requirements and accuracy. Testing to ensure robustness against a variety of datasets including documents of different lengths and types.

  • Once selected implemented in Python and containerised within a Docker image. Setup as an Application Programming Interface (API). Allowing for easy maintainability and scalability on cloud services such as AWS & Microsoft Azure or on-premise servers.

  • Reduced data required for downstream classifcation tasks by up to 95%. As well provides users with an informative glimpse into a document's topics.

  • In the example a BBC News Article is run through the key phrase extraction module. The key phrases are transformed into a word cloud for easy viewing.

Opponents of the draft directive welcomed the decision and said a new first reading of the proposals would give the EU a chance to have fuller debates about its implications in all member states. In the US, the patenting of computer programs and internet busines methods is permitted. This means that the US-based Amazon.com holds a patent for its "one-click shopping" service, for example. Critics are concerned that the directive could lead to a similar model happening in Europe. This, they fear, could hurt small software developers because they do not have the legal and financial might of larger companies if they had to fight patent legal action in court. Supporters say current laws are inefficient and it would serve to even up a playing field without bringing EU laws in line with the US.”
